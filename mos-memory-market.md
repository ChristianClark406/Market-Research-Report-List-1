<p><p>The MOS memory market is primarily divided into two types: RAM (Random Access Memory) and ROM (Read-Only Memory). RAM is volatile memory used for temporary storage, enabling fast data access for active processes, while ROM is non-volatile, preserving data even when powered off, and is typically used for firmware and boot processes. Both play crucial roles in electronic devices, with RAM emphasizing speed and performance, and ROM focusing on stability and reliability for essential functions.</p></p>
