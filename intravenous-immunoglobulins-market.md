<p><p>Intravenous immunoglobulins (IVIG) are categorized based on the immunoglobulin types they contain: IgG, IgA, IgM, IgE, and IgD. IgG is the most prevalent and is commonly used for treating autoimmune conditions and immunodeficiencies. IgA plays a role in mucosal immunity but is less frequently used therapeutically. IgM is primarily involved in initial immune responses. IgE is associated with allergic reactions, while IgD has a lesser-known function in immune response regulation. Each type serves distinct clinical applications.</p></p>
