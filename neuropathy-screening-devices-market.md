Non-electronic devices often consist of simple tools like monofilaments or tuning forks used to assess sensory function through physical examination. In contrast, electronic devices utilize advanced technology to provide quantitative measurements of nerve function and may include tools like electromyography (EMG) or nerve conduction studies. Both types play a crucial role in diagnosing and monitoring neuropathy, catering to different clinical needs and settings.</p></p>
