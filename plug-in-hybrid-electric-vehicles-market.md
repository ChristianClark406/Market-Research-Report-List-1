<p><p>The plug-in hybrid electric vehicle (PHEV) market is primarily segmented into two types: stored electricity and on-board electric generator. Stored electricity PHEVs rely on battery reserves that can be charged from external power sources for propulsion, offering enhanced efficiency and reduced emissions for short trips. In contrast, on-board electric generators utilize internal combustion engines to recharge the battery while driving, extending the vehicle's range. Both types provide a balance between electric and conventional power, catering to diverse consumer needs.</p></p>
