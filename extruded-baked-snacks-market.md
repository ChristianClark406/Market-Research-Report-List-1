<p><p>Extruded baked snacks are categorized into hot and cold extrusion processes. Hot extrusion involves applying heat and pressure to raw materials, resulting in a cooked product with a crispy texture, often used for snack foods like chips and puffs. Cold extrusion, on the other hand, utilizes lower temperatures, allowing for the preservation of sensitive ingredients and typically producing denser snacks, such as crackers or energy bars. Both methods cater to diverse consumer preferences and nutritional demands in the snack market.</p></p>
